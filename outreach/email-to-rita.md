## The letter to priests

I wrote a letter you can send to priests around New England. Many
parishes buy devotional statues, and a handwritten letter from a real
restorer is the kind of thing a pastor actually reads.

Here's what to do:

1. Print the letter below on nice paper (plain white is fine).
2. Sign your name at the bottom with a pen.
3. Tuck a photo of one or two of your favorite pieces into the envelope
   — it makes a big difference.
4. Mail one to each priest on the list at the bottom.

You can get each priest's name and address by searching online for
**"Diocese of Manchester parish directory"** (and the same for Boston,
Worcester, Portland, and so on). I'm happy to print the envelopes for
you on our next atelier day if you'd like — just say the word.

Start local and work outward. These are the dioceses that are within a
reasonable drive, so a pastor can actually come see a piece:

1. **Diocese of Manchester (New Hampshire)** — your own. Start here.
2. **Archdiocese of Boston (Massachusetts)**
3. **Diocese of Worcester (Massachusetts)**
4. **Diocese of Springfield (Massachusetts)**
5. **Diocese of Portland (Maine)**
6. **Diocese of Burlington (Vermont)**
7. **Diocese of Providence (Rhode Island)**
8. **Archdiocese of Hartford (Connecticut)**
9. **Diocese of Bridgeport (Connecticut)**

Within each one, I'd aim first at:

- Spanish-speaking parishes (they love devotional statues most).
- Portuguese parishes (New Bedford, Fall River, Cambridge).
- Traditional Latin Mass parishes.
- Carmelite, Franciscan, and Dominican communities — they're the most
  likely to want Our Lady of Mount Carmel, the Sacred Heart, and
  St. Martin de Porres.
