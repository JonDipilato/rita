My name is Rita M. Shea. For more than fifty years I have restored sacred
statuary by hand — Madonnas, Sacred Hearts, saints, crucifixes, and
reliquaries — in the old manner, in my atelier here in New Hampshire.

I am now eighty-seven, and over the decades I have gathered a small private
collection of restored pieces. Rather than see them scattered, I would like
to offer them first to the parishes and religious communities where they
will be prayed before and cared for.

The collection presently includes:

- Our Lady of Fátima, crowned — restored, with crystal-inset rosary
- Our Lady of Lourdes — vintage chalkware, rosary and mantle restored

- The Infant Jesus of Prague — jeweled crown, IHS sunburst

- Santo Cristo de Limpias — a Passion bust, rare in American parishes
- A reliquary monstrance and a set of sanctuary candle sleeves

## Who to send it to

**Priority New England dioceses (driving-distance buyers):**

- Archdiocese of Boston (MA)
- Diocese of Worcester (MA)
- Diocese of Springfield (MA)
- Diocese of Manchester (NH) — local, highest priority
- Diocese of Portland (ME)
- Diocese of Burlington (VT)
- Diocese of Providence (RI)
- Archdiocese of Hartford (CT)
- Diocese of Bridgeport (CT)

**Within each diocese, target first:**

1. Spanish-language / Latino parishes (Lawrence, Lowell, Providence,
   Brockton, Lynn, Hartford-area). Devotional statuary sells fastest here.
2. Portuguese-heritage parishes (New Bedford, Fall River, Cambridge).
3. Traditional / Latin-Mass parishes (FSSP, ICKSP, Oratories).
4. Carmelite, Franciscan, and Dominican communities (for the Carmel,
   Sacred Heart, and St. Martin de Porres pieces in particular).

**How to get the names and addresses:** Each diocese publishes a free
parish directory online (search "[diocese name] parish directory"). A Saturday
with the printer is enough to generate 40–60 envelopes.

**Follow-up:** If you don't hear in two weeks, no follow-up needed — the
letter either landed with someone who cares or it didn't. Move on to the
next diocese.
